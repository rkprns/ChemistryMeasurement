Solubility

In this part of the lab your will be looking more closely into solutions. A solution is a homogenous mixture of 2 or more substances. The substance that is being dissolved is the solute. The substance that dissolves the solute is the "solvent." Solvent and solute work very well as discriptors for a liquid solvent and a solid solute; however, if both are liquids (such as ethyl alcohol and water), or both are solids (such as bronze which is a metal alloy of coppe and tin), it is not as clear which one can be classified as the solvent vs the solute. Regardless, two solids, two liquids, or two gases can make a solution.

In this lab we will be working with liquid solvents and solid solutes. You will be finding the saturation point at different temperatures. The saturation point is the point at which no more solute can be dissolved in the solute and starts to settle out of the solution. At this point, you have turned your solution into a suspension and it is now oversaturated. At the point immediately prior to this, your solution is saturated.
